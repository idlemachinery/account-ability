Defining Accountability
Committing to doing something with a clear understanding of expectations, deciding to take responsibility, becoming empowered to overcome hurdles, and accepting of consequences and results to learn and correct future behavior.
Definitions that will change mindset
  • Accountability is taking action
  • I am personally responsible for my actions and understanding the task
  • I can be accountable for 100% of the pie... so can others
  • I am responsible for holding myself and other accountable
  • I can become empowered to do tasks assigned to me
  • External conditions do not determine my accountability level
Commonly incorrect definitions:
  1. One of the biggest mistakes we make when trying to define accountability is to associate it with blame.
  2. We tend to mathematically explain our portion of accountability as some percentage of the pie.
  3. Accountability is somehow attached to our title or salary.
  4. I don’t have the Tools, authority or Skills needed to accept a responsible role in accountability.
Committing
  • What are some things you have committed to in the past?
  • Where you always “All In” or were you sometimes “Half In”?
  • What was the difference?
  • What are the things that help us to commit to something?
Clear Understanding
  • Think of a task or even a skill that you totally understood.
  • How did you gain that understanding?
  • How do you feel when you must do something but you don’t really understand how?
  • How can we achieve clear understanding every time?
Expectations
  • How would you define Expectations?
  • How much easier is it to have success when we know what is expected?
  • What things are part of expectations?
  • Who defines expectations?
Deciding to Take Responsibility
  • How is responsibility and accountability different?
  • What makes you feel that you can be responsible?
  • If you don’t feel responsible for outcomes, will you be willing to stand up and be accountable?
  • How will taking responsibility make me more accountable?
Empowerment
  • Have you been asked to do something but felt as if your hands were tied behind your back?
  • How does commitment and responsibility affect empowerment?
  • What is Self Empowerment?
Accepting Consequences and Results
  • What is our first reaction to failure?
  • When do we want to learn about the consequences for our results?
  • How would knowing the consequences up front help us be accountable?
Learning
  • How can we turn a failure into a success?
  • Have you ever tracked failures and successes?
  • What can we learn from every consequence?
  • What can we do with all of this knowledge?
